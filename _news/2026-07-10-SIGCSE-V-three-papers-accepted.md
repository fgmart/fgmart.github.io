I'm delighted that three papers led by my students are accepted to
[SIGCSE Virtual 2026, The 2nd ACM Virtual Global Computing Education
Conference](https://sigcsevirtual2026.acm.org/):

1. **WiseSpend AI: Engaging K-12 Students in Financial Literacy by
Evaluating the Human Role in Training Artificial Intelligence** led by
J&eacute;sus Granados and Jos&eacute; Granados.

    The brothers were students in my spring [Developing AI Tools for
K&#150;12](https://utsa.simplesyllabus.com/en-US/doc/tpmey0q5r) class
where they developed an original software tool, &#147;WiseSpend
AI,&#148; which introduces students to machine learning in the context
of personal finance.

    My doctoral student John Weaver was their mentor and is
co-author. Congratulations, J&eacute;sus and Jos&eacute; for their
first conference paper!

1. **RuleRunners: A Two-Player Collaborative Software Tool for Introducing Rule-Based AI Systems to Middle School Students** by Durga Rajarajan.

    Durga reports on student learning after using
    [RuleRunners](https://pdr24.github.io/RuleRunners/login.html), an
    original game where students are introduced to rule-based AI in
    the context of playing a parkour-style side-scroller game.

    The software is designed for two players, scaffolding them to
    learn from each other and then compete to find the best set
    of rules to program an AI agent to solve the game.

    Durga's game is available now for immediate online use:
    [RuleRunners](https://pdr24.github.io/RuleRunners/login.html).
    Congratulations, Durga!

1.  **Ice Cream Express: A Game-Based Tool to Teach AI Pathfinding to
K-12 Learners** led by Nicole Tian.

    Nicole and her partner Devansh Shah developed [Ice Cream
    Express](https://icecreamexpress-6591d.web.app/), a maze game where the goal is to find the shortest path for an ice cream truck to make its home delivery. The maze is a simple grid, but some squares are blocked and others are dirt roads which slow you down.

    Players are introduced to the famous A* (pronounced
    &#147;A-star&#148;) search algorithm, which finds the shortest
    path with the smallest exploration space.

    My colleague Dr. Ismaila Sanusi coached Nicole and Devansh in
    analyzing and presenting the data on student learning outcomes and
    is co-author.

    Nicole's game is available now for immediate use: [Ice Cream
    Express](https://icecreamexpress-6591d.web.app/). Congratulations,
    Nicole and Devansh!


The papers will be available during the conference, which will be held
online November 12–15, 2026.
